Tuition & Financial Aid

Q1. Will financial aid cover all of my tuition? ▼

Each student's situation is unique. You will work with a Finance Advisor to complete a plan on how you can fund your education prior to starting classes. Filling out the FAFSA® will determine if you are eligible for federal grants and loans. This is the first step in the financial aid process.

Q2. Do I need to apply to CTU before doing a FAFSA®? ▼

You can complete your FAFSA® at any time, even prior to enrollment. The FAFSA® is the document that the Federal Government uses to determine your eligibility for different types of federal aid. On your FAFSA®, you will select which school you would like your information sent to by providing our school code. We encourage students to complete the Application for Admission first if they have indicated they are ready to move forward with CTU. This helps to ensure that the school can properly connect your FAFSA® to your student file in order to prepare and respond more quickly once we receive the FAFSA® results. Once we've received your results, your CTU Finance Advisor will be able to assist you in determining how to apply any eligible funding options that may be available to cover the cost of your program here at CTU.

Q3. I don't want to take out student loans. ▼

I understand. The decision to take out loans is important and requires your thoughtful consideration. Borrowing is a great responsibility and, as you may know, loans must be repaid along with associated fees and interest. Unfortunately, it is unlikely that other funding sources, like grants and scholarships, would be enough to cover the full cost of tuition. Our Financial Aid Department can provide you with more information about the different types of aid that may be available to you if you qualify.

Q4. I am in default on my student loan, am I eligible for financial aid? ▼

Thank you for sharing this information with me. As your Admissions Advisor, I encourage you to contact your lender, loan servicer, or collections agency in order to determine what the appropriate steps would be for you to get out of default. Would you like to speak with Financial Aid at this time?

Q5. Based on the credit load options available, will I still be eligible for financial aid if I do not choose a full time credit load? ▼

While I cannot speak to what you would be eligible to receive, I can tell you that regardless of which credit load option you choose, you will still have the opportunity to complete a FAFSA® to help determine what federal aid you may be eligible to receive. Once we receive your FAFSA®, you can talk with your Finance Advisor to determine which credit load option works best for you and your financial aid plan.

Q6. When does CTU begin charging for classes? ▼

Tuition is charged per credit hour at the beginning of each term throughout your program.

Q7. Will I have to pay an out of state tuition? ▼

No, CTU does not have different pricing for students in different states.

Q8. How will my Military Housing Allowance (MHA) be affected by attending online? ▼

Ground: Per the VA, Your MHA is based on the monthly military Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) for an E-5 with dependents. The VA starts with that amount, then takes into account additional factors such as how many credits you are taking. In general, the monthly housing allowance is calculated based on 50% of the national average for students who are attending classes solely online. You can speak with our Financial Aid Department for more details if you have questions about how your schedule may impact your MHA rate eligibility.

Online: Students attending school exclusively online would be paid at 50% of the national average for MHA. You can speak with our Financial Aid Department for more details if you have questions about how your military training time or schedule may impact your MHA rate eligibility.

Q9. Will I be eligible for FA since I'm not a U.S. Citizen? ▼

In general, individuals who are not U.S. Citizens or eligible non-citizens do not qualify for Federal Aid. To complete the FAFSA®, you will need either a Social Security Number (for U.S. Citizens), or a Permanent Resident Card, Resident Alien Card, or a green card (for eligible non-citizens).

Q10. How is the total program cost calculated? ▼

The total program cost is calculated using a normal completion time to estimate the number of terms in a degree program. Normal completion time is the way CTU estimates the number of terms to calculate the total program cost. The total program cost may vary depending on fees that are assessed each term taken. Once a student's degree plan is finalized, the Financial Aid Department can then calculate the total program cost based on the number of terms in the degree plan.

Q11. Can I apply for Federal Aid if I don't have good credit? ▼

Generally, federal student loans and other financial aid do not have a credit requirement. As a result, your FAFSA® application does not require you to include credit information. Parent PLUS Loans are the exception. In addition, if you apply for private student loans, the lender may require a credit check.

Q12. How much are the fees and what do the fees cover? ▼

Refer to the footnotes on the Tuition & Fees Schedule.

Q13. How much are the fees? ▼

Refer to the Notes section of the Tuition and Fees Schedule.

Q14. Do the fees apply to military students? ▼

For all students eligible for the military tuition rate, the book supplies and course materials charge will be posted to a student's account once each term after the add/drop period. The charge will then be waived, resulting in no net cost to the student.

For all students eligible for the military tuition rate, the Graduation & Technology Fees are not charged and therefore not included in the total program cost.

Students using VA education benefits will be exempt from the Technology Fee.

Q15. Do I have to pay the Graduation Fee if I don't attend the graduation ceremony? ▼

The fee is required of all CTU graduates, regardless of their attendance at the graduation ceremony. Although a portion of the fee is applied toward the production of the ceremony, the fee also covers the cost of one diploma and diploma cover, two official University transcripts, an honor cord (if applicable), and regalia (if attending the ceremony).

Q16. What is the tuition and program length if I'm provisionally admitted into the Doctoral of Computer Science program? ▼

The cost per credit hour for the Doctoral of Computer Science program including all 4 preparatory courses is $598 (for MIL: $540), and the total estimated program cost is $74,428 (for MIL: $64,640). This includes the fees for this program calculated at a normal completion time. Your cost may vary based on credit load.

Based on the full-time credit load which includes all 4 preparatory courses, your program could be completed in 35 months. This program length is an estimated time and varies depending on your credit load, transfer credit acceptance, academic breaks, and assumes you pass all of your courses.

Registrar Evaluation Services will review the required preparatory courses upon enrollment and may adjust your schedule.

Q38. What is accreditation? ▼

Accreditation provides educational institutions with an objectively recognized standard of quality to which they must adhere. The purpose of accreditation is to assess the educational quality of an institution, promote internal accountability and consistency with an institution's policies, promote institutional effectiveness through various assessments, and to monitor an institution's ability to effectively carry out its operations.

Q39. What is the difference between semester hours and quarter hours? ▼

CTU utilizes quarter credit hours. Quarter credit hours are approximately two-thirds of a semester credit hour in terms of course work load. Nine quarter credit hours are equivalent to approximately six semester hours. A school that operates on a quarter hour system typically requires 180 quarter credit hours to complete a bachelor's degree. A school that operates on a semester hour system, typically requires 120 semester credit hours to complete a bachelor's degree.

Q40. Is CTU a for-profit or non-for-profit school? ▼

Q41. How many students does CTU have? ▼

Q42. Do your ground campuses offer online courses? ▼

There will be some courses that are a mix and we call those blended, meaning you may be required to meet in person and virtually during the duration of your course.

Admissions & Application Process

Q44. Does the Enrollment Agreement legally obligate me to go to school? ▼

The Enrollment Agreement is a legal contract and it demonstrates your intent to start classes with CTU. However, you have the ability to rescind that contract. Please note, enrolled students are not responsible for accrued charges until after the add/drop period for each session. I encourage you to read all of the terms outlined in the Enrollment Agreement.

Q45. Can I cancel my classes whenever I want to? ▼

You have the option to cancel your enrollment through the first week of class prior to add/drop without financial obligation. After the first week you can withdraw your enrollment but will be subject to charges as outlined in your Enrollment Agreement. Additional information about the withdrawal process can be found in the University Catalog.

Q46. Why is the program length on the Program Information Disclosure different from what was shared during my interview? ▼

The amounts shown on the Program Information Disclosure differ according to the level of program you are enrolling into. Similar to traditional universities, the Associate is designed to be 24 months, the Bachelor's is designed to be 48 months, and the Master's is designed to be 18 months. What was shared during the admissions interview is a timeframe that is based on the credit load you chose and varies depending on transfer credit acceptance, academic breaks, and assumes you pass all of your courses. The program may be completed in more or less time depending on the individual student.

Q47. What is the College Financing Plan? ▼

The College Financing Plan is a consumer tool that simplifies cost and financial aid information, helping prospective students including veterans and active duty military and their families compare schools and make informed decisions. It promotes transparency and allows for easier comparison of financial aid packages across institutions.

Colorado Technical University has entered into an agreement called the Principles of Excellence with the Department of Veterans Affairs and the Department of Education. As part of this arrangement, we agree to provide information about the cost of college and estimated financial aid awards to prospective students who are veterans or active duty military and their families. The College Financing Plan must be completed prior to enrollment by individuals who select a military affiliation within the Application.

Q48. Why don't the tuition and fees on the College Financing Plan match my Enrollment Agreement? ▼

The information provided on the financing plan is estimated based on tuition and fees information that is updated quarterly. The information on your Enrollment Agreement is based on your actual enrollment. Your actual tuition costs are listed on your Enrollment Agreement. Additional fees may apply, and you are able to review the tuition and fees schedule for more detailed information on your specific program.

Q49. Why is room and board listed on the College Financing Plan? ▼

This information is included to meet the requirements set by the Department of Education. The financing plan is a universal form and "Room and Board" is a standard required allowance in the "Cost of Attendance" for each school.

Q50. I have a criminal record; can I go to school at CTU? ▼

Q52. What is the Student Tuition Recovery Fund (STRF)? ▼

This is a program for California residents required by the California Bureau for Private Postsecondary Education (BPPE) and administered by the University. It is designed to relieve or mitigate economic losses of students if the University should close. The state requires all institutions approved to operate by the BPPE to collect this fee to protect students against an unlikely closure. Students are charged a fee based on the amount of their institutional charges, which is collected by the University and submitted to the STRF program managed by the State of California. The current STRF fee is $0; the rate is subject to change at any time without notice. This amount is determined by the state and is subject to change. The fee is charged once the student starts class and is nonrefundable.

Fast Track

Q60. What score is needed to pass a Fast Track exam and how can I check my score? ▼

Undergraduate programs require at least a 70% to pass and graduate programs require at least an 80% to pass. Once you have completed all of the lessons and have a passing score, I encourage you to stop because revising the lessons may also decrease your score if you answer something wrong.

You'll be able to see your score on the Virtual Campus/Student Portal once the exam is closed and grades are posted. You'll be able to see your knowledge state throughout the exam, but this is not your final grade. The final grade will post once the exam closes.

Q61. How do I schedule a Fast Track exam? ▼

Fast Track Exams are scheduled every Tuesday and remain open for a 7-day window, closing on the following Monday at 11:59pm (CT). Once a Fast Track Exam is scheduled, it will appear in your degree plan like any other course and will have the letter "C" at the end (e.g. MATH102C). CTU recommends taking all Fast Track Exams from a laptop or desktop computer as mobile devices may not be compatible with the format in which the exam is offered.

Please note that you have one attempt per exam, and you may not reschedule an exam once it closes.

Ground: Students can schedule a Fast Track Exam by reaching out to their Success Coach.

Online: Students can schedule their own Fast Track Exam from the mobile app or Virtual Campus on the Degree Plan page. The CTU Fast Track™ icon will appear next to any course that is Fast Track eligible. You simply click on the icon and follow the prompts.

Q62. Who can I reach out to if I have questions during the Fast Track Exam? ▼

You may contact the exam facilitator through the duration of the exam. The facilitator is there to help guide students but will not be able to answer course content related questions. Depending on whether you've started your degree program, you may also reach out to me or your Success Coach. If you have technical issues, you should contact Technical Support right away.

If you need to reschedule an exam, do not log in or attempt that exam. Instead, contact Admissions or your Success Coach prior to the exam closing so it does not count as an attempt.

Q63. What happens if I pass the Fast Track Exam? ▼

If you pass the Fast Track Exam, proficiency credit will be posted to your degree plan as "PR", and the passed course will then be removed from your schedule.

Q64. What happens if I don't pass the Fast Track Exam? ▼

If you don't pass a Fast Track Exam, don't be discouraged. Fast Track Exams are not credit bearing and do not impact a student's GPA if they do not pass an exam. Any failed exam is simply scheduled as part of the student's degree program. There is not a penalty for not passing the exam.

Q65. How long do I have to take the Fast Track Exam? ▼

Once you begin an exam, you have a total of 5 hours to complete the exam during the 7-day window. Time cannot be added back or reset, but it can be paused. To "pause" or "stop" the time you must completely log out of the exam. If you do not log out of the exam, the time will continue to run for 20 minutes of inactivity. During the 7-day window, you can go back to revise any lesson you wish, if you have time left.

Q66. Are Fast Track Exams open book? ▼

No. Although you may prepare for the exam, the intent of taking a Fast Track Exam is to test your current knowledge of a particular subject.

Q67. How many times can I attempt a Fast Track Exam? ▼

You will have one attempt; however, you may revise the lessons to improve your score before your 5 hours is up.
